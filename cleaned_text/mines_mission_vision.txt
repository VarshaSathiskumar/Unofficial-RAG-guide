Mines@150
As Colorado School of Mines celebrated our 150th
anniversary in 2024, dynamic and disruptive change was all
around us. We worked towards navigating an increasingly
competitive higher education landscape, responded to the
changing education and innovation needs of industry and
society, and did not become complacent with our
accomplishments.
MINES@150 was our plan to position Mines for future
success. It called for change, but also for staying true to our
timeless mission, pillars, and core values. MINES@150
leveraged our size, location, and history, ensured that our
graduates would continue to be distinctive and highly valued,
placed Mines at the frontiers of STEM education, research,
and innovation, and elevated our status among the world’s
top universities.
Based on identified threats, a self-assessment, and campus
discussions, we recognized that to succeed as a mid-sized
and uniquely focused university, we focused on several goals.
Be a top-of-mind and first-choice university for
students, public and private partners, and faculty
and staff.

Expand offerings and diversify delivery, particularly
for professionally oriented pre- and post-graduate
education.
Grow the scale and impact of research, focus on
thematic strengths, develop a more social research
culture, and diversify funding sources.
Strengthen affinity for Mines among our students,
alumni, and external partners.
Be more innovative and entrepreneurial, especially
in the use of our resources.
Attract private investment to support our key
strategic initiatives.
Each of the values above facilitates our shared success, and
the advancement of MINES and its mission; by our examples
and by our encouragement we seek to foster these values
throughout our community, and especially among our
students, so as to inspire them to pursue excellence in our
shared lives of inquiry and innovation.
